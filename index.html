Part One

Your Smile, Your Face, You

01

Your smile. The way it shows up before you even know it's there, rearranges my whole mood, and stays in my head longer than I planned for. I'd do anything to keep seeing it. I mean that in ways I'm still finding words for.

02

Your face. The whole thing. Not just the forehead you keep showing me. Every feature, every expression, every angle — I notice. I notice everything, Precious.

03

Your beauty makes me speak in tongues — and I meant every incoherent word. Na my babe dey hot. I stand by that. Permanently.

04

Your voice. The texture of it. The warmth in it. How it sounds different when you're sleepy versus when you're excited — softer, slower, more honest — and I love both equally.

05

Who you are. Your honesty, your loyalty, your stubbornness — I'll deny I said this, but I love it. Your intelligence. Your faith. The way you carry pressure without letting the world see you buckle. You are rare. And I mean that with my whole chest.

Reason #6

The way you make me feel

You make me feel chosen. Not settled for. Chosen. You make distance feel temporary, London feel warmer, and my overthinking quieter. You make me feel like it's okay to need someone. You make silence on a call feel like a full conversation. You make me feel like the best version of myself isn't forced — it's natural around you.

07

Our conversations. How they flow without effort. The calls — even the morning and evening ones, especially the morning ones. How we discuss on WhatsApp, send snaps and whine on Snapchat, share reels on Instagram like it's a circle. How we send each other tweets and gist about them. The way we never actually say goodbye properly. We just keep talking until one person sleeps off.

08

The inside jokes. "Yes, mom." The teaching on how to use Snapchat better. The arguments and how we resolve them — you're wrong, by the way, but I love that you fight for it. Chef O. The forehead photos and how you position your camera during video calls. When I fall asleep on the call. Every single bit of it.

09

How you fall asleep on the phone and I don't hang up because the silence sounds like trust. And if you doze off mid-sentence, I'll just text you "HELLO!!!!!" and save my questions for the morning.

10

Your laugh. Not the polite one. The real one that catches you off guard, throws your head back, and takes over your entire body. The one I can hear in my memory right now.

"There is something about you that made me stop and pay attention. And the more I've paid attention, the more certain I've become."

— Olamide, to Precious

Part Two

Why I Chose You

11

Because something about you made me stop scrolling, stop looking, stop searching. You're not just someone I like. You're someone I'd show up for. Someone I'd be patient for. Someone I'd cross distances for, rearrange things for, choose deliberately and consistently. Not out of desperation. Out of something that feels very much like knowing.

12

Because you challenge me to grow without making me feel small. You make me want to be a better man without ever saying the words. The standard just rises when you're around.

13

Because you love with intention. You don't half-love anything. You don't play games — you play for keeps. That's rare, and I don't take it lightly.

The truth?

I like you, a lot. More than I've liked anyone for a long time, and to be honest, it kinda scares me. I don't wanna screw up what we have, whatever it is. I've fallen pretty damn hard for you.

14

Because loving you feels intentional, not accidental. It's made me more patient, more honest, more expressive. Words I used to swallow, I now say. You've turned me into someone who writes love letters. I didn't know I had this in me.

15

Because every alternative to you feels like settling. You've set a standard I don't want met by anyone else. You're the reason I stopped looking.

Reason #16

I notice

I notice when you're tired but still make time for me. I notice when you choose your words carefully because you care about how they land. I notice your effort — every single bit of it. I notice when you keep me updated on where you are and what you're doing, and I want you to know: that means the world. I want more of it. Not as a rule — as a quiet way of saying I matter to you.

17

The little things I adore. How you'd rather sleep than cook. The way you roll your eyes at my jokes and laugh anyway. We don't do nonchalant here. And I love that about us.

18

The way we stay up late at midnight to talk, hear each other's breath, gist and share tweets and reels and react to the wildest takes together, and somehow turn a Twitter thread into an hours-long conversation about life. You send me something with no context and I already know your commentary before you type it. That's not small talk. That's the beginning of a language only we speak.

19

I want us to build a playlist that becomes the soundtrack of us. Press play on a song at the same time and not say a word because the music says it all. Every song will have a story. We're not there yet — but I already know it'll be beautiful when we are.

20

I want to order the same meal and eat together on a video call. Cook the same recipe at the same time and argue about whose turned out better. Send you snacks from London just to see your face when you try them. Watch the same sunset from two different skies and still somehow feel like we're standing in the same place. And yes — I will drive you everywhere. But not crazy.

Part Three

My Promises & My Heart

21

If we're doing this, there needs to be a conversation between us about boundaries, intentions, and expectations. I want us to be clear. I want us to be safe. I want us to be deliberate. Not because it kills the romance — but because it protects it.

22

I promise to stay in the room when things get hard. To talk even when I'm angry. To never use silence as a weapon. To choose curiosity over defensiveness. To forgive fully and not hold the past over your head. Long-term relationships come with a lot of forgiveness. Don't think you're about to have three perfect years with someone. We all have flaws and toxic traits — so find someone you can grow and become better with. I want that someone to be you. And I want to be that someone for you.

23

For us to prosper, we must deliberately and consistently assume benevolent intent in actions, and ignorance — rather than malice — in omissions. I will lead with love. I will lead with questions. I promise to still have a crush on you on a random Tuesday. Still reach for your hand. Still light up when you walk in. Love isn't just a feeling. It's a decision. Choose me, keep choosing me, and I'll keep choosing you.

24

See me, Precious. Not just what I do, but what it costs me to do it. I don't need a parade. I just need to feel like you notice. Because a man who feels seen will move mountains. Just remind me sometimes that my best is enough.

25

Make me feel safe. I'm an overthinker, and reassurance isn't a luxury for me — it's a need. Sometimes all it takes is "I'm not going anywhere" and then living like you mean it. Love makes me happy, but safety makes me stay. Give me both.

Reason #26

Your face is a garden

And I'm its loyal gardener. That hasn't changed. It won't. Every time you smile, something in me grows. I don't know what you did to me, Precious, but I don't want the antidote.

27

I want a partner, not an audience. Notice my effort. Match it. Let's build this together. Equally. As a team. Be everything with me. My best friend, my playmate, my partner in every argument we'll ever have, my protector. We talk like we can't shut up, play like we never grew up, argue like an old married couple, and have each other's backs like nothing could come between us. That's the dream.

28

I've thought about you more than my thesis. I've smiled at my phone because of you more times than I'll ever admit. I've prayed about you. Specifically. By name. I've imagined our first hug and I already know I won't let go first.

29

You are the song engraved in my heart, and I want to sing you every single moment, forever.

30

I like you. But I'm willing to lose you. What I feel may be irrational. Staying is not. I chose this. Deliberately. I intend to stay. But if you make a fool of me, I'll leave. Desire brought me to you, but choice keeps me here. And choice can take me away. So you decide. I already have.

31

Reason #31 — The first month ended. I didn't.

Because I love you, Precious Oluwaseun. Simply. Completely. Deliberately. Today, tomorrow, and every day after that. Because you are not just someone I like. You are someone I would show up for. Someone I would be patient for. Someone I would cross distances for, rearrange things for, choose deliberately and consistently for. Not out of desperation, but out of something that feels very much like knowing. I don't care how long it takes. I want it to be you. Only you.

"One day, if you're lucky enough to find a soul steady enough to stand with you in the wilderness of love, someone who looks at you when everything seems to be tearing apart and says, 'we rebuild.' You won't just survive love itself. You'll own it."

— For us, Precious

Part Four

Beyond the First Month

32

Because I'm such a sucker for genuine love and I know that all it takes is just two committed people choosing each other repeatedly for the rest of their lives. That's it. No magic formula. No secret code. Just you and me, choosing this — choosing us — over and over again, even when it's hard. Especially when it's hard.

33

Because someone will love me unconditionally. I hope I'll find someone who loves me the way I love. And Precious — I think I found her. I think she found me too. I think the universe got tired of watching me wait and finally said, "Here. This one. She's worth every second of the wait."

34

Because Precious, you are evidence that love is such a beautiful thing, man. You make it easy to believe in something I used to be careful about. You make it easy to be soft. You make it easy to hope out loud without being afraid of jinxing it.

A quiet prayer

One day I want to be able to look at you and say:

I found my own person,
and we live happily ever after.

35

Because love is action, not just emotion. And I want to show you that every single day — in the calls I don't miss, the texts I don't leave on read, the effort I don't fake, the patience I don't run out of, and the consistency I don't let slip. Words start it. Actions prove it.

36

Because more than anything, I'm yearning for genuine companionship alongside a romantic relationship. I don't just want a girlfriend. I want someone who sits with me in silence and makes it feel full. Someone I can call my best friend and mean it. Someone who laughs at the same nonsense, fights fair, and stays through the boring bits. That someone is you.

"Unrequited love is not normal. Love must be mutual and enthusiastic. They should want you as much as you want them, or even more. What you feel about them is irrelevant if they feel nothing for you. How they make you feel is more important."

— A truth I carry with me

Reason #37

I know who I am

I'm grown enough to admit that due to the things I was put through in life, I'm a hard person to deal with sometimes. I overthink, worry, and can be hard to understand. I'm beyond territorial, and require a lot of patience. But I also know that I can love like no other. And I want to give that love to you. All of it. The messy, intense, overthinking kind — and the soft, quiet, "I'll never stop showing up" kind too.

38

Get yourself a clingy and lovey-dovey partner. Life is too short to be with people who act like it's painful to show love. I said what I said, Precious. I will be annoyingly affectionate. I will double-text. I will call you just to hear your voice. I will say "I miss you" ten times a day and mean it every single time. That's not a warning — it's a promise.

What this is for

The purpose of a relationship is to grow.
To break generational cycles
and to learn how to love —
both ourselves and another.
Its purpose isn't just to meet our needs.

It's to make us better than we were before we found each other.

39

Because a partner who says "I miss you" without being prompted, who makes it clear you live in his thoughts even when you're not around — that kind of consistency does something real. It's not just sweet words, it's emotional safety. It's feeling chosen, considered, and secure without having to ask for it. I want to be that kind of safe for you, Precious. Always.

40

Because I have zero problems having a hard conversation. I wish people weren't so scared of their feelings that they go out of their way to avoid dealing with them. Not us. We talk. We hash it out. We sit in the discomfort and come out the other side stronger. I will never run from what's hard if you're on the other side of it.

41

Because when you hurt someone and maintain a relationship with that person, they may bring it up again. They may need more clarity. Even if you've apologised, they may bring it up. And when that happens, it's on you to be patient and have a discussion about it. If you hurt that person, it's not up to you to tell them when and how to heal. I will sit with your hurt. I will be patient with your healing. These are not burdens — they're proof that I'm not going anywhere.

42

Reason #42 — The only one that matters.

Because after 42 days, I am more certain than I was on day one. Not less — more. Every conversation, every call, every silence, every argument, every laugh has added another layer of certainty. You are not a phase. You are not a maybe. You are a decision I made with my whole chest and I will keep making it. I choose you, Precious Oluwaseun. Deliberately. Consistently. Proudly. Today, tomorrow, and every day that follows. You are the reason I stopped looking — and the reason I never will again.

My Precious,

42 days. That's all it took for you to become the first person I think about in the morning and the last thought before I sleep. That's all it took for me to know.

The thing nobody tells you about real love is that it isn't the butterflies that hold it together. It's someone who is still gentle with you on a hard day. Still considerate when the newness has settled into routine. Still choosing you, quietly, consistently, without applause. That's what I want to give you. Every single day.

We've already done the hardest part — finding each other in a world full of millions.
Now let's do the easiest thing — to never let go, to never lose what we've found.

I'm choosing you over everybody. Even on the days we don't understand each other, even on the hard days and the quiet days and the days that don't make sense. I still won't play about you. Not even a little.

Yours, always — Olamide
